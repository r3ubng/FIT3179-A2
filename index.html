Netflix Library Size

This map displays the Total Netflix Library Size for
each country with available data. The shade of colour
is used to determine the quantity of the library size,
where darker shaded countries have a larger library
size whereas lighter shaded countries have a smaller
library size.

Use the slider to filter countries based off Library Size.

From the map, it is clear to see that countries such as
USA, Australia, Canada and The UK have larger
library sizes.This is most likely due to their substantial
market size, competitive streaming landscape, and
extensive licensing agreements, unlike other countries
that use the Netflix streaming service.

The bubble chart below will delve deeper into the Netflix
Library Size.

Tv Shows or Movies?
Note: Interact with bubble chart by using mouse to move or zoom.

The Bubble Chart compares the different sections of
a country's Netflix library and how it affects the total
library size.

The No. of TV shows is located along the x-axis, and
the y-axis displays the No. of Movies, whilst the Total
Library Size is represented by the size of the Bubble.
Each country is also colour coded into its respective.
continents and can be filtered by using the legend.

Malaysia is shown to have the highest number of
Movies at 2,387 but only has 3,565 TV Shows thus
having less than 6000 in Total Library Size. On the
contrary, Czechia has less movies than Malaysia at
2,091 but has the largest Library Size of our data
at 7,325 due a much larger catalogue of TV Shows.

There is a slight trend in the data where countries
with more TV Shows, have a larger Total Library Size
than countries with more movies.

The Cost of Netflix

The Stacked Bar Chart displays the Basic, Standard
and Premium subscription fee for each country. The
Basic subcription fee is encoded in orange, Standard
fee in purple and Premium in blue.

Use the bar chart located underneath as a zooming
tool to get a more detailed view for each country.

From this chart we can see the subscription fees that
each country charges, are quite similar and around the
same range. There are some outliers like Liechtenstein
and Switzerland that have Premium fees over $26 and
also Turkey with a Basic Subscription Fee of only $1.97

Population vs Cost

Unlike the preceding graphs above, These two Donut
Charts compare data between continents rather than
countries.

The Donut to the left compares the average country
population for each continent whilst the Donut chart
to the right compares the average monthly
subscription fee for each continent.

The continents can be highlighted by clicking the
continent names located on the legends. Detailed
information for each continent is presented when hovering
over a section of the Donut Chart.

We can see that continents with a higher average
population tend to have a lower monthly subcription fee,
and continents with a lower average population tend to
have a higher subscription fee in most cases.

What Makes the Most Money

This histogram heatmap, provides a final comparison
of Netflix Library size with the Monthly Subscription
Fee for each country.

The No. of TV Shows is measured on the x-axis and the
No. of Movies on the y-axis. Each country is represented
by a rectangle that is shaded darker for a higher
average subcription fee and lighter for a lower fee.

The average monthly subscription fee is calculated
from the average of the Basic, Standard and Premium
fees.

If we disregard the outliers in the bottom left of the
graph, we can see a small trend, where rectangles get
darker towards the middle and right of the graph
whereas there is no trend along the y-axis.

This means that Monthly Subcription fees are generally
higher if there are more TV shows in the Netflix
rather than more Movies.

Although a small correlation between netflix library
content and subscription fee exists, there are many
other factors that might affect how much Netflix costs.
